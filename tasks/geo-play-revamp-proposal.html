Geo Play Page — Revamp Proposal

route /:lng/geo · rev 1 · 2026-07-12 · grounded in code as of ce1af2e (v2.145.1) · audited from a live mobile capture (Half-Life, fr)

TL;DR

The free-play geo page has a solid engine (store, history, a11y plumbing, exclusion lists) wrapped in a layout that fights itself on mobile: four stacked dock rows eat roughly a third of the viewport, the primary CTA is a big gradient button that does nothing until you tap the map, and the reveal — the entire payoff of the loop — is a small transient card. The revamp keeps the store and the a11y guarantees intact and reworks the presentation in five phases: (1) collapse the dock into one contextual row and give the reclaimed space to the map, (2) turn the reveal into a tiered bottom sheet with a score count-up and guess→answer line focus, (3) add per-game progress and unify the five state screens, (4) add an optional 5-round scored "run" with a shareable recap, (5) desktop polish. Phases 1–3 are pure presentation changes; only phase 4 touches the store.

What already works well and must survive the revamp untouched:

  • The store. geoFreePlayStore handles catalog TTL, per-game played-exclusion, prev/next history (50 entries), ignore lists, real-world cold-start bias, and persists the right subset. Nothing in this proposal changes its shape until phase 4.
  • A11y plumbing. Live-region pin announcements, the coordinate-entry fallback (WCAG 2.1.1 — Leaflet can't synthesize a click on Enter), 44px targets, roving-tabindex game picker, inert map under open sheets.
  • Performance choices. Lazy Leaflet chunk, keyed transform reset on the zoomable photo, memoized slots.
  • Haptics + two-step pin (draft tick, confirm pulse).

2Problems evidence-based

P1 — The dock taxes the play surface

On the captured 780-CSS-px-tall phone, the dock (4 rows + safe-area padding) plus the top bar consume ≈240px, leaving the map — the surface the player actually acts on — about 45% of the deck. Leaflet then overlays its own zoom control on top of that. The photo panel is similarly squeezed, so both halves of the core comparison task (look at photo ↔ scan map) are cramped. The dock's height is constant even though at most one of its rows is relevant at any instant: before a pin exists the CTA is disabled; after a pin exists the skip link and coordinate toggle are hidden anyway (!revealed && !canSubmit gating in Dock).

P2 — The primary CTA lies about the next action

"Placer l'épingle" is rendered as the full-width gradient-gaming hero button, but it is disabled until a draft pin exists — the actual next action is tap the map. A saturated gradient button reads as "tap me"; a first-time player taps it, nothing happens, and there is no visible hint pointing at the map. The label then flips to "Confirmer" once a pin lands — two different actions time-sharing one button slot with no transition cue.

P3 — The keyboard fallback is promoted to primary navigation

"Placer l'épingle par coordonnées" exists for keyboard/switch/SR users and is deliberately collapsed — but its summary line is permanently visible in the dock, adding a fourth row and competing with the skip link. Sighted touch users pay the space cost of an affordance they will never use.

P4 — The reveal moment is flat

The entire loop exists for the reveal, and today it's a max-w-md card floating above the dock: static score number, "Distance : 12,3 %", pin count. No tier feedback (the design system's score-high/mid/low tokens are unused here), no count-up, no focus animation on the guess→canonical line that MapCanvasLeaflet already draws, no personal-best context. Compare the classic mode's tier reveals, which use --glow-* and count-ups.

P5 — No feedback loop across rounds

Free play is unranked by design, but it currently has zero session structure: no running total, no round counter, no per-game progress ("12/58 screenshots"), no best-score memory — even though playedByGame and screenshotCount already contain the progress data. The only long-horizon feedback is the terminal "exhausted" screen.

P6 — Chrome duplication on tiny panels

Three overlay layers stack on the panels: "Photo"/"Carte" corner badges, the top bar chips, and the top-right home/fullscreen cluster (which needed a z-index bump comment in ImmersiveLayout to stay clickable over the top bar — a smell that the header zone is overloaded). On a 380px-wide screen this is a lot of pills.

P7 — Desktop is an unexamined 50/50 split

md:grid-cols-2 gives photo and map equal width regardless of task phase. While scanning the map you want the map big; while studying the screenshot you want the photo big. Every comparable product (GeoGuessr et al.) solves this with an expandable secondary surface.

3Design principles for the revamp

  1. The map is the stage. Every pixel not doing work goes to the map (mobile) or the active surface (desktop).
  2. Show one action at a time. The dock renders exactly the controls valid in the current phase, in one row.
  3. Make the reveal the reward. Spend the animation/glow budget on the result sheet, nowhere else (ui-tokens rule: one hero glow per screen).
  4. Presentation-only until phase 4. The store contract, a11y fallbacks and E2E selectors (geo-panel-photo/geo-panel-map) are load-bearing; keep them.
  5. Tokens, not raw values. Everything maps to docs/ui-tokens.md; the design-tokens/no-raw-design-tokens lint rule is at error for src/**.

4Proposed layout

4.1 The contextual dock (mobile)

One 56px row, phase-driven — the row's contents change, its height never does (no layout shift):

Store phaseDock contents
ready, no draft pin‹ prev · shuffle · passer (skip, promoted from text link to ghost button) · next ›
ready, draft pin placedFull-width gradient "Confirmer l'épingle" + small ✕ to clear the draft
submittingSame CTA, spinner, disabled
revealedFull-width "Capture suivante" (the result sheet sits above it)
loadingRow skeleton

The disabled gradient button disappears entirely. Its job — telling the player what to do first — moves to a floating hint chip anchored bottom-center of the map panel: "Touchez la carte pour placer l'épingle" with a pin icon. It fades out on first pin of the session (persisted flag) and never returns for experienced players. When a draft pin lands, the chip position is where the confirm CTA slides in from, visually linking pin → confirm.

4.2 Where the removed controls go

  • Skip — promoted into the idle dock row as a real button. It was a dataset-quality escape hatch hidden in 12px text; making it a peer of shuffle is deliberate (a random guess pollutes contribution data more than a skip costs).
  • Coordinate entry — stays in the DOM for WCAG 2.1.1 but becomes visually hidden until keyboard-focused (sr-only focus-within:not-sr-only pattern on the details summary), the same trick as skip-links. Touch users never see it; keyboard users hit it in tab order right after the map, exactly where they need it.
  • Prev/next history — stay as icon buttons flanking the idle row. Disabled states unchanged.
  • Corner badges "Photo"/"Carte" — dropped on mobile (the panels are self-evident: one is a screenshot, one is a map with zoom controls); kept on desktop where panels are peers. Removes one overlay layer from each panel.

4.3 Context header merge

The top bar and the top-right overlay merge into a single header row (fixes the z-40 band-aid): game chip (opens picker) · map chip (multi-map only) · progress ring (new, §6) · spacer · home · fullscreen. One strip, one z-index, safe-area padded.

4.4 Desktop phase 5

Keep the split but make it 60/40 photo-dominant, with the map panel expanding to 65% on hover/focus-within and pinnable via a corner toggle (CSS grid-template-columns transition, --duration-normal). This is the low-risk version of the GeoGuessr overlay pattern — no absolute-positioned mini-map, no drag-resize, works with the existing panel DOM. The corner badges and expand affordance live in the panel headers.

5The reveal sheet

Replaces ResultOverlay with a non-modal bottom sheet (Radix primitives already in the tree via Sheet, or a plain fixed div — no new dependency):

  • Map focus. On reveal, MapCanvasLeaflet gets a fitBounds call over the guess + canonical points (it already draws the line; today the player has to find it). Wrong-map case: flash the map chip in --destructive and show the correct map name.
  • Tiered score. Count-up over --duration-slow, colored by band: ≥1500 → score-high + --glow-success; 500–1499 → score-mid; <500 → score-low. Bands align with the scoring curve in docs/geo-mode.md (2000 × e−8d: 1500 ≈ within 3.6% of target, 500 ≈ within 17%).
  • Human distance. "à 4,2 % de la cible" instead of "Distance : 4,2 %" — plus the existing pin-count line ("N joueurs ont placé cette capture").
  • Personal best delta per game, from a new persisted bestScoreByGame map (tiny, additive store field — the one phase-2 store touch, purely local).
  • A11y: keeps aria-live="polite" aria-atomic; count-up is skipped under prefers-reduced-motion (final value rendered immediately).

6Progress & session structure

6.1 Per-game progress data already exists

playedByGame[gameId].length / game.screenshotCount is already in the store. Surface it as a small ring + "12/58" in the context header and as a progress bar per game inside GamePicker (which already computes playedCountByGame). This turns the invisible march toward "exhausted" into a collection mechanic for free.

6.2 Runs — optional 5-round scored session phase 4

Free browse stays the default. A "Lancer un run" chip (header or empty state) starts a 5-round run: random screenshots across the catalog (reusing pickRandomAcrossGames), cumulative score out of 10 000, round counter "3/5" in the header, recap screen with per-round tier dots and total, and a share CTA reusing the OG-image infrastructure (og.routes.ts) so a run result unfurls nicely in Discord/X. Store gets a small run: { active, roundIndex, scores[] } | null slice; guest-visible but submit still requires auth (unchanged backend contract — runs are a client-side aggregation, zero API work).

Why bother, if GeoGamers daily mode exists? Runs answer "one more go" retention in the unranked sandbox without touching leaderboards, and they create the first shareable artifact from free play. Cut this phase first if scope pressure hits.

7Unified state screens

ScreenshotPanel currently hand-rolls five sibling states (all-done, exhausted, auth-required, error, empty) with copy-pasted icon-circle + title + body + actions markup. Extract one StatePanel (icon, title, body, actions, footnote slots) and express the five states as data. The empty state keeps its social-proof pins counter and 1-2-3 steps; the auth state gains the same screenshot behind a blur overlay ("sign in to play this one") instead of a bare panel — the strongest signup nudge is the game itself, one presentational div, no API change.

8Accessibility & i18n checklist

  • Coordinate entry: keep the form and its focus-reveal in tab order directly after the map panel; E2E-assert it still works (it's the only non-pointer pin path — WCAG 2.1.1).
  • Pin live region (output aria-live) unchanged; add a reveal announcement ("Score 1 730, à 4,2 % de la cible") to the sheet's existing live region.
  • All dock buttons keep min-h-11/12; the hint chip is pointer-events-none (decorative, aria-hidden — the live region already announces state).
  • Phase-driven dock keeps a stable DOM order: history buttons, skip, CTA — visibility toggles, not reordering, so focus never teleports.
  • Reduced motion: count-up, sheet slide-in and map fitBounds animation all collapse to instant under prefers-reduced-motion (Leaflet: animate: false).
  • New i18n keys (fr + en): geo.play.hint.tapMap, geo.play.clearPin, geo.play.result.* (tier lines, best delta), geo.play.progress, geo.play.run.*. French is primary — write fr first.

11Risks & open questions

  • Visual-regression churn. Phases 1–2 invalidate every geo snapshot; batch the test:e2e:visual:update into the same PR and flag it for reviewer eyes.
  • Focus-reveal coordinate entry must be tested on VoiceOver/TalkBack, not just desktop tab order — the details/summary + sr-only combo has browser quirks. If it misbehaves, fallback: keep it always-visible but move it into the overflow of the game chip menu.
  • Score tier bands (1500/500) are proposed from the decay curve, not play data. Validate against real pin distributions (admin has geo_pins) before hard-coding; keep bands in one exported constant.
  • Open: should the hint chip also localize for the two-step confirm ("Touchez ✓ pour valider") after the first pin, or is the CTA morph enough? Proposal assumes the morph is enough; cheap to add later.
  • Open: run length (5 vs 10) and whether runs bias toward the real-world catalog the way cold-start shuffle does. Proposal: reuse the same weightedPick untouched.
