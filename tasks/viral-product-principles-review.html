The Box vs. the 31 Principles of a Viral Product

Review packet · 2026-06-09 · audience: engineering · source: Marc Lou, "31 Principles of a Viral Product" (Just Ship It, 2026-06-09)

A pass over The Box's landing, pricing, OG, and copy surfaces against Lou's 31 patterns. These are a compass, not a checklist — and several principles are written for paid-SaaS landing pages, while The Box is a freemium daily game. Where a "failure" is actually a deliberate, defensible strategy difference, it's flagged as such rather than treated as a defect.

⚠ Code finding worth a ticket (independent of the principles)

The supporter_lifetime tier is a first-class BillingTier in packages/types/src/index.ts:1331 and is fully documented in docs/billing-stripe.md (one-time "supporter à vie" payment, dedicated webhook branch, users.supporter_lifetime_at column). But it is not present in BILLING_CATALOG (packages/backend/src/config/billing.ts) — only premium_monthly and premium_annual are. So there is no resolvable price / purchasable path for the lifetime option today. This is exactly the one-time-payment lever principle #27 argues for, sitting half-wired in the codebase. Either finish wiring it or prune the dead tier from types/docs.

Per-principle scorecard

Click any row to expand evidence + recommendation.

01No free planFAIL*

Evidence

There is an explicit Free tier (€0) on PricingPage.tsx + FreePricingCard.tsx, plus anonymous guest play of the daily challenge.

Verdict

Deliberate, not a defect. A daily-puzzle game lives and dies on top-of-funnel reach; the free daily challenge is the viral loop. Lou's "no free plan" is a B2B-SaaS rule that doesn't map. Keep the free tier; just make the premium upgrade trigger sharper (see #25, #8).

02Three colorsPARTIAL

Evidence

Design system is a dark gaming theme with purple/pink gradients plus cyan accents (OG image uses purple→pink→cyan), neon glows, glassmorphism on the premium teaser.

Verdict

The intent ("one color for the action") is mostly there — primary actions use the gaming gradient. But multiple competing accents + glassmorphism dilute focus. Note the repo's own HTML-doc rule already bans glassmorphism and caps "one neon glow per page" — the product UI is held to a looser bar than the docs. Tighten toward: dark bg, one accent for the play CTA.

03Numbers instead of adjectivesPARTIAL

Evidence — EN subtitle (home.subtitle)

"Daily video game challenges with screenshot guessing, speed-based scoring, 21 achievements to unlock, live leaderboards, and win streaks…"

Evidence — FR subtitle

"Une capture, un jeu à reconnaître. Voyons si ta culture gaming tient encore debout."

Verdict

EN does the numbers thing ("21 achievements", "7 days"). FR has zero numbers (it's pure attitude). The two locales aren't pursuing the same strategy — the FR is more memorable (#18) but less concrete. Add a number to FR; add the "45s per screenshot" hook to both (it's the most concrete, distinctive mechanic and isn't surfaced in marketing at all).

04A footer people want to shareFAIL

Evidence

Footer.tsx is legal/utility links only: Terms, Privacy, Cookies, FAQ, Game Rules, Contact, copyright + build version.

Verdict

No shareable closing moment. The natural "finish strong" asset already exists elsewhere — the daily completion messages and the OG share card. Surface a "share your result" / streak flex in the footer or post-game screen instead of ending on legalese.

05OG image = YouTube thumbnailPARTIAL

Evidence

og.routes.ts renders a 1200×630 SVG/PNG: "THE BOX" wordmark, the formatted date, tagline ("Guess the game from a screenshot."), and a CTA pill ("Play today's challenge"). Good caching, PNG fallback for Twitter/iMessage.

Verdict

Technically solid, visually generic. It's a text card, not a thumbnail with intrigue. It shows no screenshot — the entire hook of the game is "can you name THIS?" Put a (blurred/cropped) piece of today's actual screenshot on the card with a "Can you name it?" overlay. That's a thumbnail people click.

06One idea per screenPARTIAL

Evidence

Home stacks: hero, daily CTA, premium teaser, achievement teaser. The EN subtitle alone lists 6 features in one sentence.

Verdict

Section-level structure is reasonably one-idea-per-block, but the hero sub-headline tries to say everything. Cut it to one idea: the challenge. Let the lower sections carry achievements/premium.

07Headline a fifth grader understandsPARTIAL

Evidence

The h1 is literally just the brand: "The Box". Meaning is carried entirely by the subtitle.

Verdict

"The Box" is simple words but conveys nothing about the product on its own (also see #20). The OG tagline "Guess the game from a screenshot" is the real fifth-grade headline — promote it into the hero h1 (or as an h2 directly under the wordmark).

08Hard paywallFAIL*

Verdict

Intentional. Same reasoning as #1 — a hard paywall would kill the daily-habit funnel. Validation here comes from retention/streaks, not credit-card-before-data. Not applicable as written.

09Copy only you could writePARTIAL

Evidence

FR copy is distinctive and voicey ("Voyons si ta culture gaming tient encore debout"; achievement teaser "Trois trophées que tu n'as pas encore. Encore."). EN copy is a generic feature list a competitor could paste.

Verdict

The French has a personality the English lacks. Port the FR voice into EN rather than translating the feature list. The completion messages (e.g. "did you recognize at least one game?") are the strongest, most ownable copy in the app — mine that tone for the landing page.

10Show the product before explaining itPARTIAL

Evidence

Hero shows a logo + text, then a CTA. No screenshot/demo above the fold. Mitigant: guest play means the real product is one click away.

Verdict

"Show, don't tell" is half-met via instant guest play, but the landing itself tells before it shows. Drop a real (blurred) screenshot + answer field directly in the hero so the game is the hero. This also fixes #20 and #25 at once.

11Does one thingFAIL

Evidence

Classic mode + Geo mode + tournaments + referrals + premium + web push + public API/streamer kit + 2FA/passkeys. A genuine Swiss Army knife.

Verdict

This is the sharpest tension between the product and the principles. For marketing, pick one thing — "the daily game-screenshot challenge" — and let everything else be discovered after the hook lands. The breadth is fine as product depth; it's a liability only when it's the first thing a new visitor sees. Geo mode being marketed on the home nav (with an "Alpha" badge) dilutes the core pitch.

12Popcorn pricing (Good / Better / Best)PASS

Evidence

Exactly three cards: Free (€0) / Premium Monthly (€3.99) / Premium Annual (€29.99, "Most popular" badge, "~37% savings"). Annual is the anchored middle-of-attention.

Verdict

Clean three-choice layout with a highlighted recommended tier. Caveat: it's the same product at two cadences, not true Good/Better/Best feature ladders — but for a single-product game that's the right call.

13Rides a wavePASS

Verdict

Daily one-shot challenge with shareable results is squarely in the Wordle/GeoGuessr wave — a format people already understand and share. The mechanic does half the marketing. Lean harder into the "daily, everyone gets the same one" framing (it's underused in the copy).

14Steal your best copy from customersFAIL

Evidence

No testimonials, no captured user quotes, no review surface feeding copy. (Koe feedback widget exists but isn't mined for landing copy.)

Verdict

Can't write like customers talk if you're not capturing how they talk. The Koe widget is a ready-made source — pull the best lines into the landing page.

15A founder people can see and hearFAIL

Evidence

No founder face, voice, screen-recording, or personal note anywhere on the marketing surfaces. There is a Remotion marketing-video package — but it's a product promo, not a founder.

Verdict

Easy, high-leverage gap. A 30-second founder screen-recording playing today's challenge would outperform the current text hero. The Remotion pipeline is already in place to produce/host it.

16Pricing impossible to missPASS

Evidence

"Premium" lives in the header nav with an accent-pink treatment; a premium teaser card sits mid-home. Pricing is at /premium.

Verdict

Pricing is reachable in one click from anywhere and visually distinguished. Met.

17Headline people remember next dayPARTIAL

Verdict

"The Box" the name is sticky; the value headline isn't (it's a feature list). The thing people would actually remember is the dare in the FR copy. Run Lou's test — write 5 headlines around "name the game in 45 seconds", pick the one friends recall a day later.

18An emotional headlinePARTIAL

Verdict

FR nails it ("let's see if your gaming cred still holds up" — provokes ego/challenge). EN is flat and informational. Bring the emotional dare into EN; that's the "wow/wtf/laugh" Lou wants.

19Something people have never seenPARTIAL

Verdict

Screenshot-guessing is a known genre (clones exist). The novel angle is Geo mode for game maps (pinpoint where a screenshot was taken) — genuinely fresh — but it's buried behind an "Alpha" badge and gated to premium. The differentiator isn't the headline. If novelty is the goal, Geo is the surprise to lead with; if focus (#11) is the goal, keep it secondary. Pick one.

20Sellable from the hero alonePARTIAL

Evidence

Hero = wordmark + subtitle + play button. A motivated visitor gets it; a cold visitor sees a brand name and a paragraph.

Verdict

80% won't scroll. The hero needs (1) a value headline not a brand name, and (2) the product shown (a screenshot) not described. Currently it's carried by the subtitle doing too much work. This is the highest-ROI fix.

21Empathy before sellingFAIL

Verdict

No problem framing anywhere — the copy jumps straight to features. For a game the "problem" is emotional (boredom, wanting to prove gaming knowledge, daily ritual). The FR dare gestures at it; nothing develops it. Lighter weight than for SaaS, but a one-line "you think you know games?" beat would help.

22One call to actionFAIL

Evidence

Home presents many competing actions: "Play Today's Challenge", "Geo Mode" (Alpha), "History", "Play Yesterday", plus "See plans" in the premium teaser.

Verdict

Multiple paths → decision paralysis. There should be exactly one primary action above the fold: play today. Demote Geo/History/Yesterday/plans to secondary/contextual placement.

23A name people rememberPASS

Verdict

"The Box" — real words, no wordplay, easy to say/spell. Trade-off: it's generic and SEO-hostile (un-searchable, no category signal). Memorable: yes. Discoverable: no. Met on the letter of the principle.

24Sell a human desire, not a featurePARTIAL

Verdict

EN copy sells features ("21 achievements, leaderboards, win streaks"). The underlying desire is status ("prove you're a real gamer") — present implicitly in FR, never named in EN. Sell the bragging rights / leaderboard glory; the achievements are just the vehicle.

25Let people try before buyingPASS

Evidence

Guest play + free daily challenge with full core loop (10 screenshots, scoring, leaderboard) before any signup or payment.

Verdict

Strongest principle alignment in the whole app. "Play before you pay" is the default. Keep it — and make sure the upgrade prompt fires at the moment of desire (e.g. when a free user hits the 7-day archive wall).

26No weak wordsPARTIAL

Evidence

Mostly concrete. Minor hedging: "~37% savings", "From {{price}} / month", "Best value per month". No egregious "most/many/rarely".

Verdict

Largely clean. The "~" and "From" soften otherwise strong claims — fine for pricing accuracy, but the value copy could make harder statements ("the same challenge for everyone, every day").

27No subscriptionFAIL

Evidence

Both paid tiers are recurring (interval: 'month' | 'year' in BILLING_CATALOG). The one-time supporter_lifetime tier is defined in types + docs but has no catalog entry / purchasable price (see top callout).

Verdict

Lou: "one-time payments are 10× easier to sell." The lifetime/supporter one-time option is literally half-built. Finishing it would both satisfy this principle and give the "support the game" framing (already used in the pricing subtitle) a concrete product. Recommend wiring supporter_lifetime into the catalog + pricing UI, or removing the dead tier.

28CTA says what happens nextPASS

Evidence

Primary CTA is "Play Today's Challenge" / "Défi Quotidien" — specific, action-naming. No generic "Get Started". OG CTA: "Play today's challenge".

Verdict

Met. Minor: "See plans" / "Voir les offres" is weaker than the play CTA — could be "Unlock the full archive" to name the outcome.

29Don't launch without testimonialsFAIL

Evidence

No testimonials, ratings, user counts, or any third-party proof on the landing page. The achievement teaser shows aspirational locked trophies — not social proof.

Verdict

Highest-impact, lowest-effort gap alongside #15. Even "X players solved today's challenge" (a number you already have) is real-time social proof. Add player counts / streak stats / a few quotes before driving traffic.

30Describable in under 10 wordsPASS

Evidence

The OG tagline does it in six: "Guess the game from a screenshot." (FR: "Devinez le jeu à partir d'une capture d'écran.")

Verdict

Met — but the one-liner lives in the OG image, not the hero h1. Promote it (ties back to #7, #20).

31More expensive than competitorsPARTIAL

Evidence

€3.99/mo, €29.99/yr. For a casual daily game this is mid-to-low, not premium-priced.

Verdict

"Nobody talks about the second cheapest option." Pricing power for a game is real but bounded — most direct competitors (Wordle-likes) are free/ad-supported, so "charge more than competitors" is awkward here. The lifetime/supporter tier (#27) is the better lever: a higher one-time "supporter" price reframes payment as patronage rather than a cheap subscription.

Top 5 recommendations (ranked by ROI)

  1. #20 #7 #10 #30 Rebuild the hero around the product, not the brand. Lead with a value headline ("Name the game from one screenshot"), show a real (blurred) screenshot + answer box, single primary CTA. Fixes 4 principles in one change.
  2. #29 #14 Add social proof. "N players solved today" (data you already have) + a handful of Koe-sourced quotes. Cheap, high-trust.
  3. #15 Founder screen-recording in the hero. The Remotion pipeline already exists; a 30s founder clip beats the text block.
  4. #27 #31 Finish or kill supporter_lifetime. Wire it into BILLING_CATALOG + pricing UI as a one-time "supporter" purchase (and consider pricing it boldly), or remove the dead tier from types/docs.
  5. #22 #11 #9 #18 Focus the home page. One primary CTA (play today); demote Geo/History/Yesterday. Port the punchy FR voice into EN so the copy is ownable and emotional.

Scope & caveats

This review covers marketing/landing/pricing/OG surfaces and copy only — not gameplay, infra, or correctness. Quoted copy is from public/locales/{en,fr}/translation.json and may drift. Six "FAIL/FAIL*" verdicts (#1, #8, partially #27, #31) reflect deliberate freemium-game strategy that diverges from Lou's paid-SaaS framing; they're listed for completeness, not as defects. The principles are a compass, not a spec.
