1 · The angle

One thread, seven posts. Audience is the same as launch day — gamers and indie devs — plus a new segment this thread is built to reach: streamers. The promise is concrete and demoable: your daily run of The Box can appear live in your Twitch/YouTube chat and on your OBS overlay. No new product, no pricing ask — just a capability that didn't exist last week.

Keep the verified shape of the feature exact (it's a real API and devs will check): it is opt-in, read-only, and key-authenticated. It cannot submit guesses or touch another account. Three integration paths — chat command, OBS overlay, webhooks — one tweet each.

5 · What not to tweet

  • Don't link the internal docs. docs/streamer-kit.html and docs/public-api.md are noindex, senior-dev-only artifacts. Point streamers at the in-app Streamer Kit panel — that's the player-facing surface.
  • Don't call it a "developer platform" or "API product". It's a read-only convenience for streamers. Overselling invites "where are the write endpoints / SDKs" replies you can't satisfy.
  • Don't claim guess submission or write access. The API is read-only by design. Saying otherwise is both wrong and a security-flavoured claim you'd have to walk back.
  • Don't bury the API behind the changelog. The mobile nav / pinch-zoom / fuzzy-match wins are real but they are tweet 7, not the lead. One story per thread.
  • Don't post raw endpoint paths or key prefixes in the thread. "Your today endpoint with ?format=chat" reads fine; a full URL with tb_pk_live_ next to it looks like a leaked credential even when it isn't.
  • Don't promise the next roadmap item. This thread is about something that shipped. Teasing tournaments or soundtrack mode here dilutes the "we deliver what we said" point.
