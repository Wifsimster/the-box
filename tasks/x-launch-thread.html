1 · Thesis in one paragraph

The PH listing pulls a startup/SaaS crowd. Your X followers are gamers and indie devs. Don't talk to them as if they're VCs scouting deal flow — talk to them like the play test cohort they actually are. The tweet that converts on X is "here's a 5-second toy, click it", not "I shipped a product". The PH link goes in the thread, not the lead tweet — the lead is the play link. PH is the side quest. The main quest is daily-active users.

One concession to the launch: the lead tweet does say "live on Product Hunt" because that's the news today and it's where your followers can amplify with one click. Both objectives sit in tweet 1, but the verb is "live", not "vote".

11 · What not to tweet

  • "Please upvote on Product Hunt." PH actively buries launches that solicit votes. Use "live on PH" or "if it's your thing" — never "upvote", "support us", "vote".
  • DM blasts asking for upvotes. Same rule. PH staff search for "vote ring" patterns.
  • Comparison tweets vs. Guess the Game / similar products. You're the new entrant; punching down looks bad and punching up gives them free promotion. Frame as "same family, different shape" (T-table §7) and move on.
  • Tagging celebrities or big streamers cold hoping for amplification. Read as spam, lowers your reach for everyone in the thread.
  • Bragging numbers before they're real. If you're at 12 plays don't tweet "huge response". The next bad-data tweet you post will be discounted.
  • Apologising for downtime in a quote-tweet. If something breaks, reply quietly in the launch thread with "fixed in N min" once it's actually fixed. Don't pin a disaster.
  • Posting more than 3 standalone tweets in launch day. Pre-launch (§3) + launch thread (§4) + 1–2 milestones (§6) + 1 EOD wrap. That's it. Stop. The thread does the work.
  • Auto-deleting tweets that "didn't perform". Don't. The thread is the artifact. The launch is a moment that's allowed to be imperfect.
